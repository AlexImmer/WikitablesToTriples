Pokémon are fictional creatures which humans capture and train to fight each other for sport. The Pokémon franchise includes 720 revealed species of the title characters. Pokémon can be organized according to a number of different attributes. This list provides their English names and several formats of the Japanese names.

Pokémon can also be divided by the "generation" of games in which they were introduced and sorted by the other numbering systems introduced in those games. The first 151 Pokémon, introduced in Pokémon Red and Blue, have Kanto Pokédex numbers (official as of those games' remakes), which are the same as the first 151 National Pokédex numbers. Another 100 Pokémon appeared in Pokémon Gold and Silver to expand the list to 251 for the Johto regional Pokédex (to which 5 evolutions were added when the games were remade). The Hoenn regional Pokédex included 67 of these, alongside 135 new species that debuted in Pokémon Ruby and Sapphire (to which 9 evolutions were added when the games were remade;Pokémon Omega Ruby and Alpha Sapphire)but it did add many new Mega Evolutions, bringing the total to 386. Next, the fourth generation combined 81 (in Pokémon Diamond and Pearl) or 100 (in Pokémon Platinum) of the 107 new creatures from Pokémon Diamond and Pearl with 70 or 110, respectively, of the previous ones to form the regional Pokédex of Sinnoh. This was the first time not all of the newly introduced Pokémon were included in the games' regional Pokédex. Pokémon Black and White introduced 156 all-new creatures, which made up the entirety of those titles' Unova Pokédex. This leaves the grand total standing at 649. In the sequels Pokémon Black 2 and White 2, the Unova Pokédex was heavily modified; 145 species from previous entries were added and arranged into the original Unova Pokédex, bringing it to 301 species, though the grand total remained unchanged. With the release of Pokémon X and Y and its subsequent film campaigns, 71 new Pokémon have been unveiled,[a] the fewest new Pokémon in a single generation so far; however, the new Mega Evolution feature was added to the games to balance out the lack of new characters.

Pokémon may also be grouped into "families" of the Pokémon (eggs/fossils, for example) they evolve (or hatch or revive) from and into.

MissingNo.[edit]

Main article: MissingNo.
The player encountering MissingNo. in Pokémon Red

MissingNo. (けつばん, Ketsuban?), or MissingNO,[172] is a glitch only found in the video games Pokémon Red and Blue. Encountering MissingNo. is accomplished through an in-game event that causes the game to pick an error handler put in place by Game Freak to fill in spaces for non-existent Pokémon species. It gained popularity as an exploit as players discovered that the sixth item in their inventory was replicated,[173] but it caused graphical and visual errors.[172] Nintendo of America first acknowledged MissingNo. in the May 1999 issue of Nintendo Power.[174]
